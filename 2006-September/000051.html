Hi all,

I've attended the KDE world conference, aKademy 2006, in Trinity College,
Dublin. Among other things I've met there with George Staikos. George is a
KDE developer who works on security features in KDE and the Konqueror
browser. In the past he has organised meetings with developers of others
browsers about browser security, and in particular the threat of phishing
(http://dot.kde.org/1132619164/).

I talked to George after his talk on the anti-phishing framework which is
planned for KDE 4. He has heard of OPDB, though he didn't know that we
already have a Konqueror plugin. He said that the anti-phishing framework
will have pluggable backends, so basically it will be possible to use
different anti-phishing databases. He said that he would really like it if
we'd have a backend using OPDB. I told him that with libphish this should
be fairly easy. So I will look into it.
